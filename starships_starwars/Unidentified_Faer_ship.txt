A ship housed members of the Evereni family known as Faers during the High Republic Era.&#91;1&#93; Created around 679 BBY,&#91;2&#93; long before the High Republic Era,&#91;3&#93; the Faers came to inhabit the ship during the next three centuries.&#91;1&#93; At some point no earlier than 379 BBY,&#91;2&#93; while most of the eight Faers alive at that time resided on the ship, the siblings Vika and Velya lived on their own ship. At that time, the siblings met Marda Ro&#8212;the Evereni&#91;1&#93; leader of the marauder group who would eventually become the Nihil.&#91;4&#93; Ro, wanting to know more about&#91;1&#93; her&#91;5&#93; species, asked them about the Evereni. Among the things they told her was about the ship on which most of their family resided.&#91;1&#93;

Ro later recounted her encounter with the Faers and mentioned the ship in a message she recorded for her descendants.&#91;1&#93; By 252 BBY,&#91;6&#93; during his youth, Ro's great-great-grandson Marchion Ro had listened to her journal, including the message where the ship was mentioned.&#91;5&#93;

Behind the scenes[]

The ship was mentioned in the short story "A Closed Fist Has No Claws," written by Tessa Gratton and published in the 2023 young-adult anthology The High Republic: Tales of Light and Life as a part of the Star Wars: The High Republic multimedia project.&#91;1&#93;
